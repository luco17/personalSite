title: Business reading list 2026
date: 2026-07-18

| Title | Author | Description |
|---|---|---|
| *Build* | Tony Fadell | Product lessons from Apple and Nest. |
| *Made in America* | Sam Walton | Walmart’s operating system, culture and expansion. |
| *Born of This Land* | Chung Ju-yung | Hyundai’s audacious industrial expansion across sectors. |
| *Direct from Dell* | Michael Dell | Direct sales, inventory and working capital. |
| *How to Make a Few Billion Dollars* | Brad Jacobs | Acquisitions, incentives, leverage and capital allocation. |
| *Now It Can Be Told* | Leslie R. Groves | Building the Manhattan Project under pressure. |
| *Skunk Works* | Ben R. Rich and Leo Janos | Lockheed’s secret, fast-moving aircraft development organisation. |
| *Creative Selection* | Ken Kocienda | Apple’s iterative software and product development. |
| *Born to Be Wired* | John Malone | Malone’s cable empire and complex dealmaking. |
| *Cable Cowboy* | Mark Robichaux | Cable economics, leverage, taxes and Malone. |
| *Four Seasons* | Isadore Sharp | Luxury hospitality, service and asset-light growth. |
| *Robert Kuok: A Memoir* | Robert Kuok | Asian commodities, hotels and political navigation. |
| *Grinding It Out* | Ray Kroc | McDonald’s franchising, standardisation and property model. |
| *The Outsiders* | William N. Thorndike | Exceptional chief executives allocating capital unconventionally. |
| *Setting the Table* | Danny Meyer | Restaurant operations, hospitality and employee culture. |
| *Changing How the World Does Business* | Roger Frock | FedEx’s launch, financing and network struggles. |
| *Private Empire* | Steve Coll | ExxonMobil’s operations, geopolitics and institutional power. |
| *How to Make a Few More Billion Dollars* | Brad Jacobs | Scaling acquisitions, investors, culture and compensation. |
| *The Intel Trinity* | Michael S. Malone | Noyce, Moore and Grove building Intel. |
| *The Machine That Changed the World* | James P. Womack, Daniel T. Jones and Daniel Roos | Toyota’s production system transforms global manufacturing. |
| *Titan* | Ron Chernow | Rockefeller, Standard Oil and industrial consolidation. |
| *The Box* | Marc Levinson | Containerisation’s operational and economic transformation. |
| *The Prize* | Daniel Yergin | Oil’s commercial, technological and geopolitical history. |
| *Big Brown* | Greg Niemann | UPS operations, culture and Casey’s philosophy. |
| *The Frackers* | Gregory Zuckerman | Entrepreneurs who transformed American oil production. |
| *The Success Equation* | Michael J. Mauboussin | Separating skill from luck in outcomes. |
| *The Autobiography of Morris C.M. Chang*, Volumes I–II | Morris C.M. Chang | TSMC founder’s life and semiconductor career. |
| *Working Backwards* | Colin Bryar and Bill Carr | Amazon mechanisms for execution and innovation. |
